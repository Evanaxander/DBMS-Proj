Diet & Test Recommendations

Healthy Heart path

Personalized recommendations for diets, tests, and facilities by the Department of Cardiology

Our Goal

Welcome to our personalized heart health management section. We offer tailored recommendations for diet, diagnostic tests, and facilities to ensure optimal care for your heart condition.

Diet Recommendations

Foods to Include: Fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy products, nuts, and seeds.

Foods to Avoid: Salt, red meat, sweets, and sugary beverages.

Example Meal Plan:

  • Breakfast: Oatmeal with fresh berries.
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken salad with a variety of vegetables.
  • Dinner: Baked salmon with quinoa and steamed broccoli.
  • Snacks: Almonds and carrot sticks.

Foods to Include: Oats, barley, beans, eggplant, nuts, and vegetable oils.

Foods to Avoid: Saturated fats (found in red meat and full-fat dairy products), trans fats (found in many commercially baked products).

Example Meal Plan:

  • Breakfast: Smoothie with oats, banana, and almond milk.
  • Lunch: Lentil soup with a side of whole grain bread.
  • Dinner: Stir-fried tofu with mixed vegetables and brown rice.
  • Snacks: Apples and walnuts.

Foods to Include: Lean proteins, whole grains, plenty of fruits and vegetables.

Foods to Avoid: High-sodium foods, excessive fluids if advised by your doctor.

Example Meal Plan:

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt with honey and mixed nuts.
  • Lunch: Turkey and avocado wrap with a side of vegetable sticks.
  • Dinner: Grilled shrimp with a quinoa salad.
  • Snacks: Fresh fruit and low-sodium crackers.

General Nutritional Advice

Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.

Portion Control: Be mindful of portion sizes to avoid overeating.

Balanced Meals: Ensure each meal contains a mix of protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

Recommended Tests

Diagnostic Tests for Heart Health

    • Lipid Profile: Measures cholesterol levels.
    • Blood Sugar Test: Checks for diabetes.
    • C-reactive protein (CRP): Measures inflammation in the body.
    • Electrocardiogram (ECG): Records the electrical signals in your heart.
    • Echocardiogram: Uses ultrasound to examine the heart's structure and function.
    • Stress Test: Measures heart function during physical activity.
    • Continuous Blood Pressure Monitoring: Wearable devices that track blood pressure.
    • Heart Rate Monitoring: Smartwatches and other devices that monitor heart rate.
